Achilles
Symptoms
Achilles tendinopathy is a condition that causes pain, swelling, and stiffness of the Achilles tendon, the cord that joins your heel bone to your calf muscles. The Achilles tendon allows the ability to get and push off the ground, resulting when the demand placed on the Achilles tendon is greater than its ability to function. Some of the symptoms felt include heel stiffness, swelling, or tenderness.
Treatments
Achilles tendinopathy is usually caused by overuse and built up inflammation in the heel, so a break from activity is needed to fully recover from injury. Depending on its severity, a surgery could be done to treat it. Three to six months is generally the amount it takes to recover from an injured Achilles tendon.
