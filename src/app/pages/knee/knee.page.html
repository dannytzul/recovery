Runner's Knee
Symptoms
Runner’s knee is oftentimes caused due to irritation of soft tissues or inner lining of the knee, worn or torn cartilage, or strained tendons. This is essentially overuse of the knee, causing mild or severe trauma in whatever your circumstances may be. Repetitious high-impact activity is usually the reasoning for discomforting, aching pain around or behind the kneecap
Treatments
In most cases, runner’s knee can be healed over time with rest and being aware of any activity being done. No physical activity is advised while in the healing stages of this case but it can be done very lightly if necessary. If pain felt is serious, a medical visit will be the best solution so your diagnosis can be verified by a professional.
