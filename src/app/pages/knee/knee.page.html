Runner's Knee
Symptoms
Runner’s knee is oftentimes caused due to irritation of soft tissues or inner lining of the knee, worn or torn cartilage, or strained tendons. This is essentially overuse of the knee, causing mild or severe trauma in whatever your circumstances may be. Repetitious high-impact activity is usually the reasoning for discomforting, aching pain around or behind the kneecap. Runner’s knee can also be a sign of weak, unbalanced thigh muscles or problems in the feet area.
Treatments
